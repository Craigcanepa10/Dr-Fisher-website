Frequently Asked Questions

Best toothbrush?
We recommend a soft bristled brush with a head that is comfortable in your mouth. We also recommend that you brush twice daily with any fluoride tooth paste.
Do I need to floss?
Flossing is recommended once daily to help prevent cavities in between teeth and also to help reduce gum inflammation. Using a Waterpik waterflosser every night is strongly recommended.
How often do I need preventive care?
We generally recommend that you visit us twice a year for a cleaning and examination. There are other instances where we have our patients schedule treatment more frequently as periodontal conditions may exist. This is done on an individual patient basis.
Does a root canal tooth need any further treatment?
In some, not all, instances a root canal treated tooth will require a core and crown to complete treatment to strengthen the remaining tooth. Some root canal treated teeth will only require a filling.
Why does my tooth need a crown?
There are instances when a tooth is broken down enough that it requires a core filling and full porcelain crown coverage. This helps to protect the structural integrity of the tooth.
Should I get a white or silver filling?
Both restorations have a proven track record. They both work well in most instances. This is a matter of personal preference with cosmetics being the major factor in making the decision of which one to use.
Should I get a fixed bridge or removable partial denture?
Fixed and removable bridges both replace missing teeth. Fixed bridges are cemented into place and can’t be removed by the patient. Removable partials are constructed with teeth on a metal framework that is designed to be taken out by the patient.
